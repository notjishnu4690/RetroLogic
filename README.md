RetroLogic: Empowering Hardware Innovation Through Retro Computing
RetroLogic is an open-source ecosystem designed to democratize VLSI design and Verilog programming. For many students, mastering digital logic is often hindered by abstract theory and expensive, proprietary tools. RetroLogic removes these barriers by merging the nostalgic appeal of retro computing with the power of modern Field-Programmable Gate Arrays (FPGAs).
At its heart, RetroLogic is a comprehensive learning platform. By recreating classic 8-bit and 16-bit architectures, we provide a context where every gate and line of Verilog code has a tangible impact. Whether generating a VGA signal, synthesizing chiptune audio, or interfacing with a PS/2 keyboard, RetroLogic transforms complex system design into an immersive, rewarding experience.
The platform is built on accessibility. Our hardware centers on the Lattice iCE40UP5K FPGA, chosen for its affordability and support from open-source toolchains like Yosys and nextpnr. This allows anyone to dive into hardware design without costly software licenses. The board is designed for modularity, featuring Pmod-compatible headers for endless expansion and custom sensor integration.
Our first milestone—a fully documented, open-source VGA controller—provides a clear starting point for new learners. As the project grows, we will build a vast library of IP cores, in-depth tutorials, and a vibrant community of contributors.
RetroLogic isn't just about the past; it’s about building the future of hardware engineering. By making chip design accessible and community-driven, we empower the next generation of engineers to innovate and collaborate. Join us in bringing digital logic to life, one pixel at a time. Through hardware sales, premium educational content, and specialized IP cores, RetroLogic also offers a sustainable path for growth and monetization, ensuring it remains a valuable resource for years to come.
